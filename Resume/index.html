About Me

Let me introduce myself.

Welcome to my portfolio. I am currently a student at the University of the Cordilleras, rigorously pursuing my IT degree with a specialized focus on Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP). To be completely honest, ERP wasn't something I initially chose or even thought about; rather, ERP was the one that called me. I pursued this specialization to challenge myself, to expand my horizons on intricate business models, supply chain logistics, and the massive enterprise systems that keep the global economy running.

Growing up, I was never the "tech prodigy" who found passion in raw coding. The rise of AI and automated tools initially killed much of my curiosity, and for a long time, I believed that because coding wasn't my natural forte, I didn't belong in IT. However, I discovered something crucial about myself: I am a student who delivers results. I am not a "coder" in the traditional sense; I am a "Vibecoder."

What does that mean? It means that when I find a project that truly matters—whether it's a small automation tool or a complex school activity—I enter a state of hyperfocus. I don't just write syntax; I solve the problem. This shift in perspective is why I am incredibly excited about my upcoming ABAP programming courses. I am hoping to find my footing where logic meets business, eventually securing a career where I can bridge the gap between technical systems and real-world enterprise needs.

Personal Profile

I have fallen into the role of the ERP student, and I am happier here than I would be in any other specialization.

The transition wasn't smooth; due to delays in processing documents from my previous university, I was delayed by a year and spent the majority of my time at UC as an "irregular" student.
